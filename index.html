Grace Brewster Murray Hopper

US navy rear admiral and computer programmer

US Navy Rear admiral and computer programmer, Grace Hopper

A legacy to remember:

  • 1906: Born as Grace Brewster Murray in New York City
  • 1928: Graduated from Vassar college where she studied maths and physics
  • 1930: She receieved a master's degree in maths and married Vincent Foster Hopper
  • 1934: She earned a Ph.D in mathematics, an amzing feat for women at that time
  • 1943: She joined the U.S Naval Reserve due to the emergence of the World War ||
  • 1944: Grace Hopper was assigned to the Bureau of ordinance Computation project as a lieutenant
  • 1949: Her desire to continue working with computers led to her working with private industries. First, with Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation and later with Remington Rand, where she oversaw programming for the UNIVAC computer.
  • 1952: A compiler renders worded instructions into code that can be read by computer. She, along with her team created the first compiler for computer languages. This compiler was a precursor for the Common Business Oriented Language, or COBOL, a widely adapted language that would soon become very popular around the world.
  • 1986: At age 79, with 1 active years in the Navy, she retired, a rear admiral and the oldest serving officer in office.
  • 1991: Grave was awarded the National Medal of Technology, becoming the first female individual recipient of the honor
  • 1992: At a good old age of 85, she died in Arlington, Virginia and was laid to rest in the Arlington National Cemetery.

Post Death Remembrances

  • 1997: The USS Hopper, a guded missile destroyer was commissioned after her by the Navy in San Francisco
  • 2004: The University of Missouri honored Grace Hopper with a computer museum on its campus, dubbed “Grace’s Place". On display are early computers and computer components to educator visitors on the evolution of the technology.
  • Grace Hopper's legacy included encouraging young women to learn how to program. The Grace Hopper Celebration of Women In Computing Conference is a technical conference that encourages women to become part of the world of computing. The Association for Computing Machinery offers a Grace Murray Hopper Award to women who distinguish themselvesin the world of technology.
  • 2013: On her birthday, Grace Hopper was rememebered with a "Google Doddle".
  • 2016: President Barack Obama posthumously honored her with the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
